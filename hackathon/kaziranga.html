Kaziranga National Park protects the largest population of the Indian one-horned rhinoceros and a mosaic of floodplain grasslands, marshes and dense tropical forests. This page summarizes its ecology, conservation history, and visitor information.

üìç Overview

  • Location: Golaghat & Nagaon districts, Assam, India
  • Established: 1905 (wildlife sanctuary), 1974 (national park)
  • UNESCO: Inscribed as a World Heritage Site in 1985
  • Area: Approx. 430 sq km

üåø Ecology & Wildlife

  • Flagship species: Indian one-horned rhinoceros (Rhinoceros unicornis)
  • Other mammals: Asian elephant, Bengal tiger, wild water buffalo, swamp deer
  • Birds: Important wetlands for migratory waterfowl and resident grassland birds

üèõ Conservation History

  • Anti-poaching: Intensive protection and community engagement have helped rhino recovery
  • Habitat: Annual flooding by the Brahmaputra shapes the park's grassland and wetland habitats
  • Community: Local communities are active in sustainable tourism and conservation programs

üß≠ Visitor Information

  • Best time to visit: November to April (dry season)
  • Activities: Jeep safaris, elephant-back safaris, birdwatching
  • Access: Nearest towns: Golaghat, Nagaon; nearest airports: Jorhat, Guwahati

üé® Fun Fact

Kaziranga is home to more than 70% of the world's population of the Indian one-horned rhino.
